# Clinical Scenario: Nail Disorders

## Overview

Nail visits offer multiple procedure opportunities including debridement, avulsion, biopsy, and treatment of underlying conditions.

---

## Key Procedures

### Nail Debridement (11720/11721)

| Nails | Code | wRVU |
|-------|------|------|
| 1-5 | 11720 | 0.31 |
| 6+ | 11721 | 0.53 |

**Indications:**
- Onychomycosis with dystrophy
- Psoriatic nail disease
- Traumatic dystrophy

### Nail Avulsion (11730/11732)

| Procedure | Code | wRVU |
|-----------|------|------|
| First nail | 11730 | 1.02 |
| Each additional | 11732 | 0.37 |

**Indications:**
- Ingrown nail (onychocryptosis)
- Subungual debris removal
- Nail tumor access

### Permanent Nail Ablation (11750)

**wRVU:** 1.54

**Indication:** Recurrent ingrown nail, permanent removal desired

### Nail Unit Biopsy (11755)

**wRVU:** 1.22

**Indication:** Melanonychia, nail tumor, diagnosis of nail disorder

## Common Scenarios

### Onychomycosis Visit

**Billing:**
- 99213 + G2211 = 1.63 wRVU
- If debridement (6+ nails): + 11721 = 2.16 wRVU

### Ingrown Nail

**Billing:**
- 99213-25 + 11730 = 2.32 wRVU
- Bilateral: + 11732 = 2.69 wRVU
- Permanent: 99213-25 + 11750 = 2.84 wRVU

### Melanonychia Evaluation

**Billing:**
- 99214-25 + 11755 = 3.14 wRVU

---

## Medicare G0127

For dystrophic nail trimming in patients with:
- Diabetes with neuropathy/PVD
- Peripheral vascular disease
- Other conditions affecting wound healing

**wRVU:** 0.17

**Documentation:** "[Systemic condition] requires professional nail care due to risk of injury with self-care."

## "Next Time" Points

- Nail biopsy (11755) for melanonychia → often missed
- Document diabetic/PVD status for Medicare
